Every watch drives something, and this one drives a Golf GTI. The Casio MTP-VC01 looks like a plain steel dress watch from any distance that matters, which is the entire point of it. The digital display sits inside the dial and stays out of the way until you ask it for something. Nobody notices it across a table, and the watch is not asking anybody to.

<p class="pullquote">Two things that look ordinary on purpose.</p>

The GTI runs the same play at a much larger scale. It wears the shape of a car that carries groceries, and underneath that shape is an engine that will embarrass things costing twice as much. The restraint is deliberate rather than accidental. A car that looks fast and is fast has told you everything before you turn the key, while a car that looks ordinary and is not ordinary keeps something back for the person driving it.

That is the whole match. Both objects hide their best feature in plain sight, and both reward the small number of people who know it is there. Being underestimated is not a flaw either one is trying to fix.

I will name the fight before somebody else does. The honest objection is that the GTI stopped being a true sleeper years ago, because the badge is famous now and most people can read it. That is fair, and the answer is that the GTI is still the version of the idea most people can actually reach, which is the same argument the VC01 makes on the wrist. If you want to hand the sleeper title to a Civic Si instead, the comments are open, and I would rather have that argument than agree quietly.
